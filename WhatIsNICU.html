All new parents anxiously await the birth of their child and the day that they will be able to take it healthy at home... For some parents, however, the day that their baby is born is the day that they hear and learn for the first time what NICU is.

    The birth of a premature baby or a baby who needs special care is usually unexpected for the parents. For most of the parents it is a frightening experience  that fills them with anxiety and questions. Getting answers to these questions directly and accurately , can facilitate this unprecedented situation and help parents to offer more to their baby.

So what is NICU?

The  abbreviation indicates Neonatal Intensive Care Unit and stands for a specific part of the hospital where newborn babies are transferred when they need intensive, specialized and personalized care in order to overcome some health problems. In an NICU there are advanced technology machines and trained personnel to help both the children with severe problems who are at risk of their lives and babies who are not at risk but for whatever reason, they need special care for some time. The purpose of NICU is to stabilize the functions of the infant so that it will be able , subsequently, to grow and evolve smoothly with its own forces.

The first time that a parent visits NICU is usually an experience that looks like entering another world. The special instruments, the sounds, the smells, are all unknown and foreign, the doors are closed and controlled, parents need to wash their hands, need to put on special gowns and masks to enter , even the staff seems to speak another language.   

Learning something about what is happening there, will let you focus on your baby. You must not forget that you can play a crucial role in your baby’s care, even when it is in NICU.
